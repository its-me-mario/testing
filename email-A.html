POD reaches 50,000 vaccinations, in-person commencement, and other updates
To view this email as a web page, go here.

Dear Students and Colleagues,

This week’s University Status Update briefing gave us the opportunity to share several pieces of good news, including the University POD reaching an incredible milestone of delivering its 50,000th dose of COVID-19 vaccine. This is an outstanding testament to the hard work and dedication of our team, and I am very proud we are providing this necessary service to our community. 

We shared many other key updates, including the announcement that we will celebrate the Class of 2021 with a series of in-person ceremonies tentatively scheduled from Tuesday, May 11, to Tuesday, May 18. Currently envisioned for students-only participation on an optional basis, the ceremonies also will be streamed live and recorded so that families and friends can participate remotely. Provost Folks and I shared more details on Monday, and updates will be provided at commencement.arizona.edu. 

Of course, by no means have we forgotten our Class of 2020. The Presidential Events and University Ceremonies team is exploring options to celebrate them in a distinct way, as many have requested.

Other highlights from the briefing include: 

  • Instructional Stage: We remain in Stage 2 for instruction, and we anticipate continuing so the week of March 15 as well. 
  • Testing: From March 1 to March 6, we administered 11,242 COVID-19 tests, with 29 positives, a positivity rate of 0.26%. Check the dashboard for the latest data.  
  • Vaccine POD: We are now serving those aged 65 and older, as well as other 1A and prioritized 1B community members. If you are eligible and have not yet been vaccinated, please go to podvaccine.azdhs.gov to make an appointment. Call (602) 542-1000 if you need help. Bilingual schedulers can assist you without the use of a computer. We expect that all faculty and staff will be able to be vaccinated this spring, and we are committed to ensuring students can be vaccinated swiftly as soon as they are eligible. 
  • Fall 2021: We intend to provide an in-person learning experience for those students who want it. We plan to return most courses at the main campus to fully in-person formats, with some courses offered as flex in-person as appropriate for public health needs. Live online and iCourse options will remain available for students who need or wish to continue learning remotely. 

This week marks the one-year anniversary of our decision to move to remote instruction in Spring 2020 as we began adapting procedures for the COVID-19 pandemic. While it is hard to believe this has been our reality for a year, the progress we have made with vaccine delivery and mitigation procedures is outstanding. Keep masking up, washing your hands, and staying physically distant. I also encourage you to see the CDC’s recent guidelines for those who are fully vaccinated.

If you would prefer more information on the press conference, you may either read the detailed summary at University News, or you may view the recording of the briefing. 

Bear Down, Mask Up, and Vax Up,

Robert C. Robbins, M.D.

President
The University of Arizona
